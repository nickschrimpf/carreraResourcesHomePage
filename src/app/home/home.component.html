Bridge loans provide interim financing to allow investors and owners the flexibility to overcome credit or liquidity constraints and complete projects that don’t meet typical lending parameters or timelines. These short-term loans range from $1M to $10M+ and are well suited for:

  • Renovating or rehabilitating a property
  • Repositioning an asset
  • Balloon note payoff
  • Short-fuse acquisitions
  • Offset cash flow gaps during lease up
  • Partner buy-out
  • Recapitalization

Bridge loans are faster and easier to obtain, but the short-term nature and higher risk mean they are more expensive than traditional bank financing. In many situations, a bridge loan may be the only option that meets immediate project needs and the higher interest rates and origination points are justified by the time constraints and value-add proposition of the deal.

Debtor-in-Possession Financing

Carrera Resources, Inc can provide unique structured funding solutions for real estate owners and operators who are facing interim balance sheet issues.

Debtor in possession (DIP) financing is a special form of financing granted to companies in financial trouble. Usually these companies are in a Chapter 11 bankruptcy. The unique feature of a DIP loan is that the bankruptcy court usually grants a super-priority status to the new loan. This means that the new loan gets to jump in front of any mezzanine financing and any senior mortgages in the debt stack.
